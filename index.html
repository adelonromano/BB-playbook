02
ONBOARDING OPENER SCRIPT SOP
Written · Not Vibed · Consultative Tone · Lock This Before Next Call
1Cherish the Win
"Hey folks, great news — Ethan sent us over his notes. Seems like he feels you qualify as ideal partner profiles. You identify as ambassadors. Great to know. We've worked with 150 groups to date and we've got a really good plan based on how you guys are set up."
AD's exact framing. Reference Ethan by name — signals a tight handoff, not a cold restart.
2Frame the Housekeeping
"Just to make sure we don't put you on the wrong activation success plan — let's go through a couple of housekeeping items. Here's everything I already have an understanding of."
AD's exact words. The plan is the reason for the pre-fill — not a deliverable you announce at the end.
3Pre-Fill Known Info
"Based on your call with Ethan: [X agents], Google Workspace, lead-gen focus — I've already pre-loaded all of that. I'm not going to waste your time re-asking what Ethan already captured."
Read directly from your pre-call form. They should hear their own details reflected back — proof the team is one unit.
4Ask Only What You Don't Have
"Here are the specific questions I do want to open up — [1–2 gaps from your pre-call form]."
Max 2. If you need more, your pre-call form wasn't filled properly.
5Open-Ended Close
"Is there anything else I'm missing — or anything important for you and me to know to onboard your team successfully?"
Ethan's line, AD endorsed it. Open-ended. Silence is fine. This is where you learn what the sales call didn't surface. Listen more than you talk.
